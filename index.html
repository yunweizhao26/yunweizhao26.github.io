Yunwei Zhao

Welcome! I am a second-year Ph.D. student of Computer Science at NYU Courant Institute of Mathmatics advised by Lakshmi Subramanian . Previously, I was an undergrad at the University of Washington, working with Richard Anderson and Sheng Wang.

My research theme is Human-AI Reasoning in Large-Scale Networks and Systems. I focus on building autonomous agents capable of complex reasoning over massive, noisy, and unstructured real-world data. Towards this goal, I am currently tackling two key problems: 1. Network Inference: Reconstructing gene regulatory networks from highly sparse single-cell data using Bayesian contrastive learning. 2. System Verification: Scaling policy-compliant reasoning to verify large-scale software systems using neuro-symbolic architectures.
